Discover the Wild Atlantic Way

The Wild Atlantic Way, 1600 miles (2600 km) in length, is one of the longest defined coastal routes in the world. It winds its way all along the Irish west coast from the Inishowen Peninsula in the north down to the picturesque town of Kinsale, County Cork, in the south.

Along the way, you will encounter dramatic sea cliffs, pristine sandy beaches, lively towns, and tranquil hidden bays. The route guides you through a rich tapestry of natural beauty, ancient history, and vibrant local culture. Discover medieval castles, ancient monasteries, and charming villages where Irish traditions and hospitality come alive.

Whether you are seeking adventure, breathtaking landscapes, or moments of peaceful reflection, the Wild Atlantic Way offers something for every traveler. Take your time to savor the fresh Atlantic air, enjoy hearty local cuisine, and immerse yourself in the warm welcome of communities that dot the coastline.

Prepare for an unforgettable journey filled with spectacular views, outdoor activities, and the true magic of Ireland’s Wild Atlantic Way — a route that stays with you long after you return home.

Although the cable cars are now mainly a tourist attraction, they’re still used by local commuters to get to and from work. The California Street line is particularly popular among commuters on weekdays.

Join us on the coast


The Wild Atlantic Way is divided into 14 distinct stages that showcase diverse landscapes and experiences along Ireland’s west coast. From the rugged Northern Headlands through the scenic Bay and Cliff Coasts, down to the picturesque Southern Peninsulas and Haven Coast, each stage offers unique discovery points, charming towns, and cultural highlights. This segmenting allows travelers to explore the route at their own pace, whether taking on a full road trip or enjoying shorter excursions focused on specific regions.
